## 1. Column Glossary & Financial Explanations

Here is a breakdown of the columns in our `credit_risk_dataset.csv` and their business definitions:

| Column Name | Data Type | Description | Key Financial Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **`loan_id`** | Integer | Unique identifier for each loan. | The **Primary Key**. In production, this must be unique. |
| **`member_id`** | Integer | Unique identifier for the borrower. | The **Foreign Key** pointing to a customer profile. |
| **`loan_amount`** | Float | The principal sum of money requested/approved. | Represents the company's financial exposure. |
| **`interest_rate`** | Float | The annual percentage rate (APR) charged on the loan. | Determines the revenue generated by the loan. |
| **`grade`** | String | A risk rating class (e.g. A, B, C, D, E, F, G). | Assigns creditworthiness. 'A' represents lowest risk (highest rating); 'G' represents highest risk. |
| **`loan_status`** | String | The current state of the loan repayment. | Indicates default risk (e.g. Fully Paid, Current, Late, Charged Off). |
| **`purpose`** | String | The borrower's stated reason for the loan. | Used to build risk profiles (e.g. 'debt_consolidation', 'credit_card', 'car'). |
| **`annual_income`** | Float | The borrower's self-reported annual income. | Crucial for calculating debt repayment capacity. |
| **`dti`** | Float | Debt-To-Income ratio. | Defined as: $\frac{\text{Monthly Debt Payments}}{\text{Monthly Income}} \times 100$. A higher DTI indicates a higher risk borrower. |

## 2. In-Depth Financial Concept Breakdown

### `loan_status` & `default`
*   **What is a default?** A default occurs when a borrower fails to meet the legal obligations of their loan (i.e., stops making payments).
*   **How does it map to status?**
    *   `Fully Paid`: The borrower paid off the loan + interest. (No risk).
    *   `Current`: The borrower is paying on time. (No risk).
    *   `Late`: The borrower missed a payment. (High risk of defaulting soon).
    *   `Charged Off`: The borrower stopped paying for a prolonged period (usually 120-180 days). The bank has declared the debt uncollectible and written it off as a loss. **This is a default.**
*   *In our upcoming analytics and cleaning phases, we will engineer a derived boolean column `is_default` where status is `Charged Off` or `Late`.*

### `interest_rate` & `grade`
*   **Risk-Based Pricing:** Financial institutions charge higher interest rates to higher-risk borrowers to compensate for the likelihood of default.
*   **Mapping:** Grade `A` loans (low default chance) will have low interest rates (e.g., 5-8%). Grade `G` loans (high default chance) will have high interest rates (e.g., 20-28%).

### `dti` (Debt-To-Income)
*   If a borrower earns \$10,000/month and has \$4,000/month in existing debt payments, their DTI is **40%**.
*   **Industry Benchmark:** Most banks refuse to issue unsecured personal loans to borrowers with DTIs exceeding **45-50%** because it indicates they are over-leveraged.

## 3. Dataset Profiling Analysis

We executed our profiling script `scripts/profile_dataset.py` and discovered the following critical anomalies in our raw CSV dataset:

1.  **Missing Values (Nulls):**
    *   `emp_length` has 97 nulls (1.94%).
    *   `interest_rate` has 61 nulls (1.22%).
    *   `annual_income` & `dti` have 45 nulls (0.9%).
    *   *Action:* We must handle nulls. (e.g. dropping them, or imputing them with averages or default values).
2.  **Primary Key Violations:**
    *   There are 50 duplicate `loan_id` records.
    *   *Action:* In data warehousing, primary keys must be strictly unique. We must implement deduplication during cleaning.
3.  **Outliers and Corrupt Records (Logical Violations):**
    *   `loan_amount` has a minimum value of `-4930.00` (Negative loan amounts are logically impossible!).
    *   `interest_rate` has a maximum of `97.48%` (Usury laws cap personal loans, and a 97% rate is an outlier).
    *   `dti` has a maximum of `148.04%` (A DTI above 100% means debt payments exceed total income—mostly invalid or extreme anomalies).
4.  **Categorical Anomalies:**
    *   `grade` contains an invalid grade `X`.
    *   `loan_status` contains an invalid status `Unknown`.
5.  **Data Type & Format Issues:**
    *   `issue_date` is read as an `object` (string) rather than a date, and contains mismatched formats (some `YYYY-MM-DD`, some `MM/DD/YYYY`).
    *   *Action:* Parse dates and cast types during cleaning.
